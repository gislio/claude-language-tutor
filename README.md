**Version**: 1.0
**Date**: March 2026
**Purpose**: This guide explains how to install and use the Language Tutor agent pipeline — a comprehensive AI-powered language learning system grounded in evidence from 31 books on language acquisition, polyglot research, and current SLA (Second Language Acquisition) science.

## What Is Language Tutor?

Language Tutor is a system of five specialized AI agents that work together to help you learn any language as efficiently as possible. The system draws on research from polyglots like Kató Lomb, Benny Lewis, Gabriel Wyner, and Boris Shekhtman, as well as academic research on second language acquisition by scholars like Stephen Krashen, Paul Nation, and Merrill Swain.

The system supports **any language** — from widely studied languages like Spanish, French, and Mandarin to less common languages like Icelandic, Swahili, or Welsh. It adapts to your current level, your available time, and your personal learning goals.

## The Five Agents

### 1. langtutor-assessor — Your Starting Point

**What it does**: Assesses your current language proficiency and creates a personalized learning plan.

**When to use it**: At the very beginning of your language learning journey, or whenever you want to reassess your level after significant progress.

**How to trigger it**: Say things like:
- "I want to learn Spanish"
- "Assess my French level"
- "Create a language learning plan for Japanese"
- "I'm a beginner in German — where do I start?"
- "How good is my Portuguese?"

**What it produces**:
- A CEFR level assessment (A1 through C2) for each skill area (reading, writing, listening, speaking, vocabulary, grammar)
- A personalized learning plan with milestones, daily practice recommendations, and estimated timelines
- A learner profile stored in your workspace that all other agents reference

**Important**: Always start here. The other agents read the profile and plan that this agent creates.

---

### 2. langtutor-session — The Core Tutor

**What it does**: Conducts interactive language learning sessions of any length (5 to 60 minutes).

**When to use it**: Whenever you have time to practice. This is the agent you'll use most frequently.

**How to trigger it**: Say things like:
- "Give me a 15-minute Spanish lesson"
- "Practice French conversation with me"
- "I want a grammar lesson in German"
- "Teach me some Italian vocabulary"
- "Quick 5-minute review session"
- "Let's do a cultural deep dive in Japanese"

**Session types available**:

1. **Vocabulary Builder** — Introduces new words using multi-modal encoding (sound, meaning, mnemonic connections, context sentences, collocations). Based on Gabriel Wyner's research on memory.

2. **Grammar in Context** — Teaches grammar through pattern discovery rather than rule memorization. You see examples first, then extract the pattern — following Kató Lomb's principle that "grammar should be learned from language, not language from grammar."

3. **Conversation Simulation** — Interactive role-play dialogues (ordering at a restaurant, job interviews, social gatherings, debates). Uses Boris Shekhtman's communicative tools including "islands of safety" and bridging techniques.

4. **Listening & Comprehension** — Develops listening skills through dictation exercises, comprehension questions, and shadowing guidance. Follows a progression from slow/clear speech to natural speed.

5. **Reading Practice** — Graded reading passages with vocabulary extraction, comprehension questions, and cultural notes. Calibrated to your CEFR level.

6. **Review & Reinforcement** — Spaced repetition review of previously learned material. Mixes old and new content (interleaving) for optimal retention.

7. **Cultural Deep Dive** — Explores the culture behind the language — customs, idioms, social etiquette, media recommendations. Because language and culture are inseparable.

**How sessions are structured**:
- Warm-up (10%): Review previous material, greet in the target language
- Core Lesson (60%): New material with guided practice
- Active Practice (20%): Interactive exercises applying what you learned
- Cool-down (10%): Summary, preview of next topics, progress update

### 3. langtutor-vocab — Vocabulary Master

**What it does**: Manages all vocabulary learning using a scientifically-optimized spaced repetition system (SRS).

**When to use it**: When you want focused vocabulary work — learning new words, reviewing existing words, or generating vocabulary lists.

**How to trigger it**: Say things like:
- "Review my Spanish vocabulary"
- "Teach me new French words about food"
- "Quiz me on my German vocabulary"
- "Create a vocabulary list for travel in Japanese"
- "How many words have I mastered?"
- "Flashcard practice"

**Key features**:

- **Spaced Repetition System**: Based on the SM-2 algorithm (the same science behind Anki and SuperMemo). Words are scheduled for review at optimal intervals — just before you would forget them. Research shows this produces 40-50% higher retention than cramming.

- **Multi-modal Word Introduction**: Each new word includes pronunciation (IPA), meaning, a mnemonic device, example sentences, collocations, and related word forms. This elaborative encoding creates multiple memory pathways.

- **Frequency-based Priority**: Words are prioritized by how common they are. The most frequent 1,000 words cover approximately 80% of everyday conversation — so you get maximum communication ability for minimum effort.

- **Varied Exercises**: Not just flashcards. Includes fill-in-the-blank, multiple choice, cloze tests, sentence creation, and translation exercises. Variety prevents boredom and strengthens different types of recall.

- **Progress Statistics**: Tracks total words learned, mastery rates, accuracy trends, and upcoming reviews.

### 4. langtutor-tracker — Progress Dashboard

**What it does**: Analyzes all your learning data and provides comprehensive progress reports with actionable recommendations.

**When to use it**: Periodically (weekly is ideal) to see how you're progressing and what to focus on next.

**How to trigger it**: Say things like:
- "How am I doing in Spanish?"
- "Show me my language learning progress"
- "What should I focus on this week?"
- "Am I improving?"
- "Progress report"
- "When will I reach B1?"

**What it provides**:
- Overall CEFR level estimate with skill-by-skill breakdown
- Vocabulary statistics (total words, mastery rate, review accuracy)
- Session history analysis (total time invested, session types completed)
- Streak tracking (consecutive days of practice)
- Milestone progress with estimated completion dates
- Skill balance analysis (identifies if you're neglecting any skill area)
- Personalized recommendations for what to study next
- Motivation support based on your achievements

**Why it matters**: Research consistently shows that visible progress is one of the strongest motivators for sustained language learning. Regular check-ins with the tracker help you stay on course and celebrate wins.

### 5. langtutor-immersion — Material Generator

**What it does**: Creates downloadable study materials tailored to your level and interests.

**When to use it**: When you want materials for self-study outside of interactive sessions — reading practice, worksheets, reference sheets, or conversation preparation.

**How to trigger it**: Say things like:
- "Create a reading passage in Spanish about cooking"
- "Generate a grammar reference sheet for French verb conjugations"
- "Make me a conversation scenario card for ordering at a German restaurant"
- "I need a cultural guide for Japanese business etiquette"
- "Create study worksheets for Italian at B1 level"
- "Generate a travel phrasebook for Portuguese"

**Material types**:

1. **Graded Reading Passages** — Stories and articles calibrated to your CEFR level with vocabulary glossaries, comprehension questions, and cultural notes. For beginners (A1-A2), includes interlinear texts with word-by-word translation beneath each line.

2. **Conversation Scenario Cards** — Detailed role-play guides with vocabulary, key phrases, cultural etiquette, and conversation flow suggestions.

3. **Grammar Reference Sheets** — Concise, pattern-based grammar references with examples and practice exercises.

4. **Cultural Immersion Guides** — Deep explorations of customs, food culture, social etiquette, idioms, and media recommendations.

5. **Study Worksheets** — Comprehensive exercises combining vocabulary, grammar, reading, writing, and translation.

6. **Phrasebooks** — Practical phrase collections for travel, business, social situations, emergencies, and professional domains.

All materials are saved as downloadable files in your workspace.

## Getting Started: Your First Session

Here is the recommended sequence for getting started:

**Step 1**: Start with the assessor. Say: *"I want to learn [language]"* — the assessor will ask you questions about your current level, goals, and available time, then create your personalized learning plan.

**Step 2**: Begin tutoring sessions. Say: *"Give me a 15-minute [language] lesson"* — the session agent will read your plan and suggest an appropriate lesson type, or you can request a specific type.

**Step 3**: Do vocabulary work. Say: *"Review my [language] vocabulary"* or *"Teach me new words about [topic]"* — the vocab agent will use spaced repetition to build your word bank efficiently.

**Step 4**: Check your progress. Say: *"How am I doing in [language]?"* — the tracker will show you a comprehensive dashboard of your progress.

**Step 5**: Get study materials. Say: *"Create a reading passage in [language] about [topic]"* — the immersion agent will generate materials you can use for self-study.

**Repeat Steps 2-5 daily** for best results. Research from polyglots and language scientists consistently shows that 30-60 minutes of daily practice produces far better results than longer but less frequent sessions.

## How the Data System Works

All your learning data is stored in your workspace folder under:

```
Language-Tutor/learner-data/[language]/
├── profile.json    — Your CEFR levels, goals, preferences, native language
├── plan.json       — Your learning plan with milestones and timeline
├── sessions.json   — History of every session (date, type, duration, topics)
├── vocab.json      — Your complete vocabulary with spaced repetition data
├── progress.json   — Aggregated progress metrics, streaks, milestones
└── materials/      — Generated immersion materials
```

This data persists between sessions. Each agent reads and writes to these files, so your progress accumulates over time. The agents coordinate through this shared data — for example, when langtutor-session introduces new vocabulary, it writes to vocab.json so langtutor-vocab can schedule reviews.

## Tips for Maximum Learning Efficiency

These recommendations are distilled from 31 books on language learning and current research:

**Consistency over intensity**. Thirty minutes every day beats three hours once a week. Every polyglot in the literature emphasizes daily practice as the single most important habit.

**Speak early and often**. Don't wait until you feel "ready." Use conversation simulation sessions from week one. Even imperfect communication builds confidence and reveals gaps in your knowledge.

**Prioritize high-frequency vocabulary**. The first 1,000 words cover roughly 80% of daily conversation. Focus on learning the most common words first through the vocab agent's frequency-based system.

**Balance your skills**. Use the tracker to ensure you're developing reading, writing, listening, speaking, vocabulary, and grammar in parallel. Neglecting any skill area creates weaknesses that slow overall progress.

**Engage with culture**. Language and culture are inseparable. Use the cultural deep dive sessions and cultural immersion guides to understand not just what to say, but when, how, and why.

**Embrace mistakes**. Every polyglot describes mistakes as learning tools, not failures. The agents are designed to correct gently and help you learn from errors.

**Use spaced repetition faithfully**. When the vocab agent schedules reviews, do them. The spacing algorithm is optimized to maximize retention with minimum effort — but only if you follow the schedule.

**Read extensively at your level**. Request graded reading passages from the immersion agent and read in the target language as much as possible. Reading is the single most effective way to build vocabulary in context.

## Understanding CEFR Levels

The system uses the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR) to track your progress:

**A1 (Beginner)**: Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions. Can introduce yourself and ask/answer basic personal questions. Vocabulary: ~500-1,000 words.

**A2 (Elementary)**: Can communicate in routine tasks. Can describe your background, immediate environment, and matters of immediate need. Vocabulary: ~1,000-2,000 words.

**B1 (Intermediate)**: Can deal with most travel situations. Can produce simple connected text on familiar topics. Can describe experiences, events, and ambitions. Vocabulary: ~2,000-4,000 words.

**B2 (Upper Intermediate)**: Can interact with native speakers with reasonable fluency. Can produce clear, detailed text on a wide range of subjects. Can explain viewpoints on topical issues. Vocabulary: ~4,000-8,000 words.

**C1 (Advanced)**: Can understand a wide range of demanding texts. Can express ideas fluently and spontaneously. Can use language flexibly and effectively for social, academic, and professional purposes. Vocabulary: ~8,000-12,000 words.

**C2 (Mastery)**: Can understand virtually everything heard or read. Can summarize information from different sources. Can express yourself spontaneously, very fluently, and precisely. Vocabulary: ~12,000+ words.

## Estimated Time Investment

Based on research from the Foreign Service Institute (FSI), polyglot memoirs, and SLA studies:

| Transition | Estimated Hours | At 30 min/day | At 60 min/day |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|---------------|
| 0 → A1   | 100-150 hrs    | 7-10 months   | 3-5 months    |
| A1 → A2  | 150-200 hrs    | 10-13 months  | 5-7 months    |
| A2 → B1  | 300-400 hrs    | 20-27 months  | 10-13 months  |
| B1 → B2  | 400-500 hrs    | 27-33 months  | 13-17 months  |
| B2 → C1  | 500-600 hrs    | 33-40 months  | 17-20 months  |
| C1 → C2  | 600-800+ hrs   | 40-53 months  | 20-27 months  |

These are estimates for languages of moderate difficulty for English speakers (e.g., Spanish, French, German). Languages more distant from English (e.g., Mandarin, Japanese, Arabic, Korean) may take 1.5-2x longer. Languages closely related to one you already speak may take significantly less time.
